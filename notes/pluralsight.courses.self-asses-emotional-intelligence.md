# Pluralsight Course: Self-assessing Your Emotional Intelligence

## Recognizing High Emotional Intelligence in Others

* high emotional vocabulary
  * honesty about emotions
  * can articulate those emotions
* SASHET
  * sad
  * angry
  * scared
  * happy
  * excited
  * tender
* Robert Plutchik's Wheel of Emotions [^1]
  * willingness to thank, apologize and praise
    * reason for apology and format of apology
  * Resilience and persistence when encounter setbacks
  * willingness to say "no"
    * knowing when to do so

## Realizing how you react to other people

* positive emotional triggers
  * what conditions or people cause feelins of
    * contentment?
    * happiness?
    * excitement?
* negatively connoted emotional triggers
  * what conditions or people cause feelings of
    * anger or frustration?
    * sadness?
    * fear or anxiety?
* times that anger -> outward aggression
* times that sadness -> pessimism or despair
* times that fear/nervousness -> anxiety/avoidance
* tools
  * website apps
  * differential scale
    * track daily to see change over time
    * `difficulty admitting weekness` <--|--> `difficulty admitting strengths`
  * framing: "weakness --> "opportunity for improvement"
  * what things outside of work impact emotional state at work and vice versa?
  * which things in the past impact today
